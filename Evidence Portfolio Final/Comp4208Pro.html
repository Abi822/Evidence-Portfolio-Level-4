Professional Practice

Learning outcomes assessed

  • Demonstrate understanding of the IT business environment including: the cultural context of business.
  • Develop a project plan based on a given brief including: End-user training and documentation; Resource allocation and management; End-user requirement/s; Milestones, timelines, resources, deliverables, work schedule; planning tools.
  • Demonstrate knowledge of Lifecycle methodology including: ongoing maintenance, security and development.
  • Describe ethical and legal considerations within the IT business environment.
  • Demonstrate relevant communications for an organisation at a professional level, for example: oral and visual presentations, structured report writing (document testing, evaluating, feedback, feedforward).

Assignment Description

Almost all small businesses and home offices use computers to perform their daily operations. The users of these devices don’t have the skills, the time or the desire to do their own computer service, repair and upgrades, and that puts a computer technician right in the middle of one of the fastest-growing industries in the world. Companies in computer repair industry are involved in repairing and maintaining one or more of the following

  • Consumer electronic equipment
  • Computers
  • Office machines
  • Communication equipment and
  • Other electronic and precision equipment3
