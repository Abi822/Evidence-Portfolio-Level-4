The aim of this course is to enable students to demonstrate both professional capability and an understanding of the IT environment.

Learning Outcomes

On successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate understanding of the IT business environment including: the cultural context of business.
  2. Develop a project plan based on a given brief including: End-user training and documentation; Resource allocation and management; End-user requirement/s; Milestones, timelines, resources, deliverables, work schedule; planning tools
  3. Demonstrate knowledge of Lifecycle methodology including: ongoing maintenance, security and development.
  4. Describe ethical and legal considerations within the IT business environment.
  5. List item
  6. Work effectively with others
  7. Demonstrate relevant communications for an organisation at a professional level, for example: oral and visual presentations, structured report writing (document testing, evaluating, feedback, feed forward).

Course Topics

  • Business analysis –understand business environment and the cultural context of business
  • Planning tools my include: mind mapping, PMI, flow charts, Gantt charts, PERT charts
  • Ethical considerations includes:
  • Acceptable use’ policies of the organisation
  • Intellectual property
  • Creative Commons, copyright
  • Treaty of Waitangi/Te Tiriti o Waitangi
  • Social/cultural inclusiveness
  • Gender imbalance
  • Code of Practice
  • Legal considerations includes:
  • Compliance with regulations, legislation, internal policies
  • Existing and proactive anticipation of impact of changes to legislations and regulations
  • Confidentiality, privacy, standard professional conventions, copyright
  • Code of Professional Conduct
  • Organisational code of conduct
  • Working effectively with others includes:
  • Working collaboratively
  • Some responsibility for others
  • Self- management

Learning and Teaching Overview

Careful consideration has been given to the content of the course to ensure material is presented in a manner best suited to achieving the aims and learning outcomes of the course. This course is based on common subject areas, which incorporate a number of different learning outcomes.

Theoretical and Practical Components

This course is delivered using traditional face to face teaching with a significant amount of applied learning, often in the computer labs.

Off-campus Delivery

Any off-campus learning will be carried out in accordance with Toi-Ohomai health and safety procedures.

Health and Safety Requirements

All learning activities will be carried out in accordance with Toi-Ohomai health and safety procedures.

Indigenisation

Concepts of Māori world views, traditions and protocols will be integrated and contextualised throughout the teaching and learning activities of the course as appropriate. This course will encompass the Toi-Ohomai values including a significant focus on whakapono, whanaungatanga and manaakitanga through an inclusive, respectful and supportive learning environment.

Internationalisation

Respectful, inclusive and collaborative engagement with a broad range of cultures is a fundamental part of working in an increasingly globalised world. Teaching and learning throughout this course will incorporate international perspectives, and ensure the identity and culture of all students is embraced within the learning environment.

Sustainability

An awareness of sustainability concepts and sustainable practices relevant to the computing context will be integrated as appropriate throughout the teaching and learning activities of this course.

Literacy and Numeracy Demands

Development of numeracy and literacy skills will be will be integrated throughout the teaching and learning activities of this course as appropriate in relation to the computing context and applications. This course will require students to: become familiar with relevant terminology; read and interpret information from course material and other texts; present information in writing.

Technology Enabled Components

A range of electronic resources will be used to support teaching delivery, and student learning and assessment. These resources include: Toi-Ohomai’s Moodle, web links and other sources of information.

Employability and Integrated Work Experience

A range of skills that will enhance student employability will be integrated throughout the teaching and learning activities of this course including time management, self-organisation and initiative, punctuality, interpersonal communication and interaction, teamwork, personal presentation, critical thinking and problem-solving, work ethics, and knowledge of real-world work expectations and requirements in relation to the computing industry context.
